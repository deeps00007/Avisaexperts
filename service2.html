Visa Information

WORK VISA

A work visa is a legal document that allows a foreign national to live and work in a specific country for a designated period. It typically requires sponsorship from an employer and is issued for various employment types, including skilled labor, seasonal work, or specific professions. Work visas may have different eligibility criteria, application processes, and conditions, depending on the host country’s immigration laws. They are essential for ensuring that foreign workers can contribute to the economy while complying with local regulations.

VIA-VISA

A transit visa is a type of visa that allows travelers to pass through a country en route to their final destination. Typically, it is required for those who have layovers or connections in a country but do not intend to leave the airport or stay for an extended period. Transit visas can vary in duration and requirements, depending on the country’s immigration policies. They are usually easier to obtain than other visas, as they are designed to facilitate short-term travel. Travelers should ensure they meet the specific conditions set by the transit country to avoid any travel disruptions.
